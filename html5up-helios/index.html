About Me

I am born and raised in Escondido, CA, a small town in North County San Diego. When I turned 21 I sold all of my belongings including my beloved 1967 Chevy Camaro and embarked on my life journey. I started off by moving to the tropical islands of hawaii and lived the chill surfing life under the sun for 3 years after realizing that I wanted to start a real career somewhere and do something that would excell me in life. That being said I sold my things once more and picked a city on the map. Out of a random pick I realized I had never been to Seattle before and decided to move there.

Up until this point I had always worked in the hospitality business and wanted a career. After seeing some close friends that are software engineers here, I watched them code and was instantly hooked. I've always loved computers and this was my chance to actually make something with them and tell them to do things for me. I started off by teaching myself through books, codeacademy and treehouse then applied to DevBootcamp. I then got accepted and started a new adventure and started my career transition.

DevBootcamp is an intense 19 week course that is full time. The average day was around 12 hours or more just living, eating and breathing code. We learned so much in such a short amount of time that it was amazing.
